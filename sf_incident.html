San Francisco Incident Analysis Project (Work in Progress)

Mapping and Analyzing San Francisco Incident Data
Python, MySQL, Tableau

PROJECT DASHBOARD

GOAL

Improve understanding of San Francisco and increase public safety by identifying high-risk areas and times through data analysis, and provide recommendations for reducing incident rates.

QUESTIONS TO ANSWER

Crime Pattern Analysis:
1. Identify the most frequent types of incidents based on the incident description.
2. Analyze incident occurrences by police district or neighborhood to identify areas with higher crime rates.
3. Determine the most common times (hourly, daily, or weekly) for incidents to occur.

Hotspot Analysis:
1. Visualize incidents on a map using latitudes and longitudes to identify geographic hotspots of crime.
2. Calculate the density of incidents in different intersections, police districts, or neighborhoods to pinpoint areas with a high concentration of incidents.

Time Series Analysis:
1. Analyze incident data over time to identify temporal patterns and seasonality in crime rates.
2. Examine changes in incident frequencies during specific time periods (e.g., weekends, holidays) to detect patterns.

Social Network Analysis:
1. Explore connections between incidents by analyzing incident descriptions to identify potential links between offenders or modus operandi.
2. Investigate any relationships between incidents occurring in the same neighborhood or police district.

Predictive Analytics:
1. Build predictive models using historical incident data to forecast future crime occurrences in specific locations or time periods.
2. Utilize features such as incident type, location, and time to predict the likelihood of incidents.

Resource Allocation Optimization:
1. Analyze incident severity and location data to optimize the allocation of police officers, emergency services, or other resources.
2. Identify high-demand areas where additional resources may be required based on incident frequencies or incident types.

Comparative Analysis:
1. Compare incident patterns across different police districts, neighborhoods, or time periods to identify disparities or trends.
